The Challenge

In remittance companies, feature delivery involves heavy coordination between Product Owners (defining requirements), Designers (crafting UI/UX), Compliance Teams (ensuring regulatory adherence for KYC, AML, and money transmission laws), and Engineering (building and deploying). Each handoff introduces delays — requirement ambiguity, compliance review cycles, and design-to-spec translation gaps slow time-to-market for critical features.

Core Problem: Non-engineering stakeholders depend entirely on engineering bandwidth for spec translation, prototyping, compliance documentation, and iteration — creating bottlenecks that compound across every handoff.

Key Friction Points

The traditional workflow suffers from three systemic issues. First, ambiguous requirements — POs write PRDs in natural language that engineers must interpret, leading to misalignment discovered late in the cycle. Second, serial compliance reviews — compliance teams review artifacts only after they're "done," meaning feedback triggers full rework loops. Third, engineering as bottleneck — non-technical teams cannot prototype, generate compliance documentation, or validate specs without waiting for engineering capacity.

What Changes

Parallel workstreams replace serial handoffs. Each team uses Cowork independently to produce their artifacts simultaneously, rather than waiting for the previous stage to complete. Specs arrive engineering-ready because Cowork helps POs think through edge cases, API structures, and acceptance criteria upfront. Compliance is embedded early — regulatory requirements are generated alongside the spec, not reviewed after the fact. Prototypes replace static mockups — designers produce clickable HTML prototypes that engineers can inspect, reducing interpretation errors.

Key Takeaway

Cowork does not replace any team's role — it gives each team the ability to independently produce higher-quality artifacts, faster, without waiting for engineering bandwidth. The result is that when Engineering begins building, they receive a complete, validated, and aligned package rather than an ambiguous spec that triggers weeks of back-and-forth. For remittance companies operating under strict regulatory timelines and competitive pressure, this shift from serial handoffs to parallel, AI-assisted preparation can meaningfully compress delivery cycles.

Rate Limits & Usage Resets — Key Details

Per-member limits, not team-wide: Each team member has their own independent usage quota. If one member hits their limit, it does not affect anyone else on the team. Limits reset on a rolling 7-day cycle from the start of each member's session. Premium seats carry two separate weekly limits — one across all models and a second dedicated to Sonnet models — providing significantly more headroom for sustained, high-volume use.

Extra usage (overage): Admins can optionally enable extra usage so members are not blocked when they hit their weekly cap. Overage is billed at standard API rates and is subject to per-user and org-wide spending caps set by the admin. If extra usage is not enabled, members simply wait for the 7-day reset.
